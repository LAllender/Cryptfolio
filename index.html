Article

Cryptocurrency is virtual money that is secured by cryptography. There are no central authorities like banks or governments that manage and maintain its value. It is a form of digital asset that is distributed across large networks of computers. Transactions are recorded in blocks that are linked together in a chain of previous transactions, called blockchain. The most popular and well-known versions are Bitcoin, and Etherium but there are thousands of different crypto in circulation. It has no physical form like paper money or coins. Owners keep it in a digital wallet and buy or sell through online exchange. Cryptocurrency can be used to buy regular goods and services but mostly they are used as investments and bought and sold like stocks or precious metals. It is a highly speculative investment, with the potential for intense price swings.
